Sample Screens from XAnalytics

The image slider below shows examples of what XAnalytics looks like, with data from a real course. In this case, the course is the second run of Super-Earths and Life from 2015. Click the top images to navigate, or click the bottom ones for a larger view.

The top of the XAnalytics dashboard shows basic course facts: number of registrants, number of people who have "viewed" or "explored" the course, the amount of various types of content in the course (video/html/problem), etc.

This screen shows how many views there have been in each course section ("chapter" here). Clicking on the chapter name will take you to a more detailed view as shown in the next image.

Here we see detailed information about each piece of the course, including some simple statistics for each assesment. Clicking on the assessment takes you to a screen that shows response histograms and even individual student responses (not shown here).

A graph of student activity over time. The bar on the bottom allows you to zoom in on a particular timescale. The scale is more granular than in Insights, showing day-by-day activity. You can also see one particular day with missing data, where the activity is reported as zero.

A graph of enrollment over time. XAnalytics can show not just general enrollment, but also enrollment for specific groups, such as those who completed the course.

A graphical representation of enrollment by country. XAnalytics also shows a table with this information. Nothing in XAnalytics is presented purely graphically - you can always find a table and/or download the raw data.

Enrollment map by state for the USA.

Percentage of course-viewers (note: not enrollees) by UN-designated geographical region. This is an excellent example of a custom report, which can be tailored to a particular subgroup of students and shown in a variety of ways.

IRT discrimination and difficulty table for the assessments in Super-Earths. Uses a 2PL model by default.

IRT characteristic curves for each item in the course. A planned feature is to make this a more interactive display, with roll-over information available for each curve.

A plot of discrimination versus difficulty for all the problems in Super-Earths. This plot shows roll-over information for each dot, making it easier to determine which items are the outliers.

A plot of average problem score vs. IRT difficulty for each problem. A characteristic S-curve shape can be seen, with some potential outliers on the bottom right-hand side.
