Optimal Scheduling

The queueing model provides a theoretical framework for calibrating your overall review frequency budget and individual deck review rates to accommodate different arrival rates of new items and item difficulties. Formally, the model can be used to specify a nonlinear optimization problem that maximizes throughput subject to budget and stability constraints (where the exponential forgetting curve is used to model routing probabilities in the Leitner system), which we can solve to find throughput-optimal review rates.

The model can also identify a system that is near instability (i.e., contains an almost-overwhelming number of items), and gives intuitive mechanisms (e.g., flushing and reneging) for alleviating stress on the system.
