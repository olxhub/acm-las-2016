In order to gain a better understanding of the learners’ experience, we applied the holistic framework [7] and aggregated demographic information from both the Coursera demographic Survey and in-house surveys to enhance our characterization of the learners 

Regarding the survey respondents, we used Margin of Error (ME) and response rate to indicate the sample accuracy of the respondent pools. The sample sizes satisfied a 2% ME threshold, and response rates of the pre-course surveys are much higher as opposed to those of the post-course surveys.

In general, our learners tend to submit responses to the pre-course survey towards the beginning of the course, and do the post-course survey around the ending weeks. The pattners in the four MOOCs are displayed in the following figures.

There are a few interesting numbers emerged from these figures when one tries to connect them with the actual survey implementation approaches in each of the MOOCs - 

  • LTTO recorded the highest number of responses in the pre-course survey as it was fully embedded in the first course activity.
  • In INTSE giving credits for participation made a difference not only in the cumulative responses over time, but keeping the post-course survey open from the start allowed learners to complete the MOOC at any time supporting the idea that MOOC is not a course.
