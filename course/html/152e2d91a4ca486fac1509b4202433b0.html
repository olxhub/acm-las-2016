The ASSISTments TestBed Workflow

1) Ideally, researchers come to the ASSISTments TestBed with a formulated, or partially formulated research plan. After learning about the capabilities and general implementations of studies within the TestBed, researchers can tweak their designs to fit within the system.

2) The second step actually requires developing the study within ASSISTments, using simple authoring tools to add new content or to alter pre-existing content. Researchers are advised to design their studies within a single problem set, most commonly a Skill Builder (or mastery learning based assignment), which helps to disseminate the study broadly.

3) In the third step, the study is implemented and made 'live' for users. When researchers wish to use the ASSISTments subject pool, the final version of a study is reviewed by members of our research team to validate that the content can be placed in our "ASSISTments Certified" folder. If the study is not a Skill Builder but researchers still wish to use the ASSISTments subject pool, we can also try to help establish an 'orchestrated study' with participating teachers (note that this reduces sample sizes drastically). For those using their own subject pools, these levels of added validation by the ASSISTments team are not necessarily required.

4) After a study has been running for a period of time (acceptable sample sizes are usually accrued within 1 to 6 months) researchers receive automated data reports from the Assessment of Learning Infrastructure (ALI). More information on ALI will be provided in the next section.

5) Like our researchers, we hope that the final outcome of a study is a publication, but we realize that not all studies 'work' or observe significant or novel results. In some cases, researchers may wish to repeat steps 1-4 to revise and re-implement their study ideas.
