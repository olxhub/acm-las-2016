Pate (1993) acknowledged the complexities of student satisfaction and categorised the literature on student satisfaction into three perspectives:

  • a psychological-wellness type satisfaction,
  • a job-type satisfaction
  • a consumer-type satisfaction.

These could also be arranged on a timeline continuum in which the ‘psychological-wellness’ is related to personal characteristics of the students and their previous experiences. The ‘job-type’ satisfaction projects into the future and represents aspirations, hopes and career development associated with the college experience, while the ‘consumer-type’ is associated with students’ daily interactions with the institution.
