The goal of this flipped course is to provide you background on this work, and invite your feedback and questions, as well as future collaboration in ongoing research.

The central resource for discussion and activity at the flipped session will be a Google Document, which you can access by typing into your browser the URL tiny.cc/axisgdoc. Try opening it now. It also shows the planned activities for the flipped session.

Please write your questions, feedback, or any topics for discussion into tiny.cc/axisgdoc as you go through this content, during the session in Edinburgh, or anytime you have a thought.

This flipped course provides you:

Page 1: The abstract and paper (this page).
Page 2: An abbreviated overview of the key points in the paper-like what you'd get in a talk.
Page 3: Resources and opportunities for discussion of how this system and the underlying MOOClet technology could be useful in other areas.
Page 4: Additional Resources & Materials: New directions for AXIS, how the MOOClet technology is being used.

The paper, embedded below, can also be downloaded at tiny.cc/lspdf, and can be cited as:

Williams, J. J., Kim, J., Rafferty, A., Maldonado, S., Gajos, K., Lasecki, W. S., & Heffernan, N. (2016). AXIS: Generating Explanations at Scale with Learnersourcing and Machine Learning. Proceedings of the Third Annual ACM Conference on Learning at Scale.
