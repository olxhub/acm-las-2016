Alan is a Professor in Human-Computer Interaction at the HCI Centre, University of Birmingham and Senior Research Scientist at Talis (also based in Birmingham), but, when not in Birmingham or elsewhere lives in Tiree a remote island off the west coast of Scotland, where he organises a twice-yearly maker/meeitng event Tiree Tech Wave.

As well as numerous articles on many aspects of human–computer interaction and related areas, Alan is an author of  one of the major international textbooks on Human-Computer Interaction.

His interests are eclectic: formalisation and design, physicality and digitality, the economics of information, structure and creativity, and the modelling of dreams. His commercial work has ranged from intelligent interface technology in the dot.com years, to submarine design, and intelligent lighting that may turn the world very kitsch.

In the gaps between this, he walked around Wales in 2013 (over 1000 miles) and is completing a new book, TouchIT, on physicality and design.
