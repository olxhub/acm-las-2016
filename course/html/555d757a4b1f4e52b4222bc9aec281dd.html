Quick evaluation tools

A number of opportunities to gather participants’ feedback while participants engage with the MOOCs were crafted specifically to avoid disrupting the interaction.

The figures below presents two formative evaluation approaches – within lecture video survey (IVS in INTSE) and outside lecture video survey (Quick Evaluation in P2P). The three IVS questions were designed to evaluate students' perceived effectiveness of the course lecture content. Quick Evaluation not only adopts the 5-point scale used in the IVS questions but also featured space for the learner to indicate unsatisfactory areas of the content. The intention was to integrate the opportunities for feedback in a 'light' and less obtrusive way, avoiding over-surveying participants.

The intention of designing two approaches was to understand the tool effectiveness in each case. Polls and end-of-module evaluation focused on content knowledge (multiple-choice questions designed to diagnose students’ understanding of a topic, or positions towards a controversial topic).
