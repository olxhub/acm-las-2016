Surveys and questions

Surveys and student evaluation of teaching are used widely in higher education in order to gauge feedback from students to inform the evaluation of learning, teaching and quality [7, 8]. In this section we describe the instruments in more details, focusing on the rationale and questions asked.

A rolling ‘demographic survey’ was deployed from Coursera starting in 2012 – which was 18 months before any of our courses started in order to capture time independent information – gender, age, qualifications, etc. These details are used in Coursera’s own dashboard to provide stratified detail to institutions. We used this as a key data source, to complement other aspects captured.

Additional in-house surveys (pre-coure and post-course) were developed in collaboration between academic leads and the university’s Learning and Teaching unit. To capture a range of aspects, maximize response rates and measure potential changes in learner perception, each MOOC implemented pre and post course surveys, together with additional evaluation tools.

Each instrument was created to reflect differences in the learning design of each course, but a set of common questions was maintained across at least two MOOCs to allow for comparisons.

In general, the pre-course surveys focused on motivation, prior experience, intention of engagement, and the expected learning outcomes.

The post-course surveys explored satisfaction, content effectiveness, personal development and the possible discrepancies between intentions and actual engagement during the course.

It is worth noting that a common block of course experience questions was used in the post-course surveys across three MOOCs. The questions were adapted from the on-campus end-of-semester evaluation with the intention to enable some benchmarking of the course experiences of learners in MOOCs and on-campus courses and to test the effectiveness of specific questions with the large number of participants in the MOOCs.
