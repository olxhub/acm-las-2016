While MOOCs and other forms of large-scale learning are of growing importance, the vast majority of tertiary students still study in traditional face-to-face settings.  This paper examines some of the challenges in attempting to apply the benefits of large-scale learning to these settings, building on a growing repository of cross-institutional data.

This work is driven by the particular data available and systems developed at Talis, and our work on addressing the challenges, but the lessons are likely to apply equally to providers of VLEs and other widely used educational software.

Two videos take you through the main points of the paper:

  • What is Scale? – Looks at the vast scale of face-to-face learning in tertiary education.
  • Challenges of Diversity – Discusses the diverse data we encounter at Talis handles, and some of the challenges of diversity arising: heterogeneity of courses, individual study patterns of students and cross institutional issues of ownership and privacy.

However, this is work-in-progress and since writing the paper there has been some progress!   A third slightly longer video describes this:

  • Trace Analysis – early results –  describes very preliminary results looking at trace data from a Talis Lighthouse pilot..
